Frequently Asked Questions

Where will the events be held?

The events will be held in 3 physical locations and 1 virtual location.

What does "Full participation is required" mean?

The workshops will run from 9am to 5pm on September 26 & 27 and 9am to 3pm on September 28. Due to the highly interactive nature of these workshops, attendees must be present for the full duration of the meeting. Partial attendance will not be rewarding for the attendee and will likely be disruptive for other attendees. Please plan to arrive on the evening of September 25 and to depart on the evening of September 28. The virtual workshop is no exception. Attendance at the virtual scoping session will require focused participation and the ability to ignore your inbox ;)

Does the "Full participation" requirement apply to the virtual workshop?

Yes. Just like the physical events, each segment of the virtual event will build on the previous activity and so full participation will be required. If you choose to attend the virtual event, please clear your calendar as you would if you were attending a face-to-face event.

Will travel costs be covered to attend one of the in person workshops?

Yes, travel and lodging costs will be provided for each selected participant to attend a regional, in-person workshop (virtual workshops can be attended from the participant's home or office and don't have associated costs).

When will I know if I have been selected to participate in a workshop?

We anticipate notifying all applicants of their status by August 27, 2018.

What is NSF looking for in applicants?

NSF is looking for a broad range of disciplines, institution types and career levels. NSF is also looking for diverse participation to reflect the multicultural communities of our nation's coastlines.

Are the Great Lakes part of the coastlines that will be discussed at these workshops?

Yes! Individuals with expertise on coastal research focused on the Great Lakes are encouraged to apply.
